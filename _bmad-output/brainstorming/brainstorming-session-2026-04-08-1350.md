## Phase 1: Expansive Exploration — Cross-Pollination

Ideas generated by transferring patterns from other domains to Starterskalender.

### Domain 1: Project Management (Jira, Asana, Monday.com)

**[PM #1]**: **Kanban Board for Onboarding**
_Concept_: Visual drag-and-drop board where each starter moves through phases (Pre-boarding → Day 1 → Week 1 → Month 1 → Completed). HR and managers see at a glance where each starter is in the process.
_Novelty_: Shifts from task-oriented to journey-oriented thinking.

**[PM #2]**: **Task Dependencies / Chain Tasks** ⭐ (User highlight)
_Concept_: Tasks that only start when a previous task is completed. E.g., "Create IT account" can only begin after "Contract signed" is checked off. With automatic notification to the next responsible person.
_Novelty_: Prevents people from doing work that isn't relevant yet.

**[PM #2b]**: **Chain Tasks with Fan-out**
_Concept_: Tasks can have a "blocks" relationship. One task can unlock multiple successors (fan-out). Visual: a mini-workflow per starter showing which tasks are waiting on what. Configurable per entity/role in task templates.
_Novelty_: Goes beyond simple ordering — models real business processes with parallel and sequential steps.

**[PM #2c]**: **Template Chains**
_Concept_: In admin, you can link task templates as chains: "When Template A completed → automatically create Template B and C." Configure the photo→business cards→badge flow once and it works for every new starter.
_Novelty_: Zero manual work after initial configuration — the chain "knows" what the next step is.

**[PM #3]**: **Workload Balancing**
_Concept_: Dashboard showing how many open onboarding tasks each employee has. Warns when someone is overloaded and suggests redistribution.
_Novelty_: Shifts focus from "is it done" to "can it be done."

### Domain 2: CRM / Sales Tools (Salesforce, HubSpot)

**[CRM #4]**: **Onboarding Pipeline with Phases**
_Concept_: Each starter goes through defined phases (Pre-contract → Contract phase → Pre-boarding → Active onboarding → Completed). Per phase, the right tasks and responsible persons are automatically activated — builds directly on PM #2.
_Novelty_: Makes the entire lifecycle visible and measurable, not just loose tasks.

**[CRM #5]**: **Starter Score / Health Indicator**
_Concept_: An automatically calculated "onboarding score" per starter: how many tasks on time, materials delivered, documents signed. Red/orange/green indicator on the dashboard.
_Novelty_: Shows problem cases at a glance without having to scroll through tasks.

**[CRM #6]**: **Automated Follow-up Sequences**
_Concept_: If a task is X days overdue, automatically escalate: first reminder → then manager notification → then HR admin alert. Configurable per task type.
_Novelty_: Elimination of "forgotten tasks" without manual follow-up.

### Domain 3: E-learning / LMS (Moodle, Cornerstone)

**[LMS #7]**: **Onboarding Checklist with Progress Bar**
_Concept_: Each starter (or their manager) sees a visual progress bar: "Onboarding 65% complete." Broken down by category (IT: 100%, HR: 50%, Facilities: 0%). Provides motivation and overview.
_Novelty_: Gamification element that increases engagement from all parties.

**[LMS #8]**: **Self-Onboarding Portal for the Starter**
_Concept_: The new employee gets access to a personal portal: welcome message, own tasks ("upload photo", "choose parking spot"), documents to read, who's who. Engaged before day 1.
_Novelty_: The starter is no longer a passive "object" but an active participant in their own onboarding.

**[LMS #9]**: **Certification / Check-off with Proof**
_Concept_: Some tasks require "proof" — an upload, a signature, a screenshot. E.g., "Safety protocol read" requires digital confirmation. Extends beyond Epic 3 (Confidentiality) to broader compliance.
_Novelty_: Compliance-ready onboarding with audit trail per action.

### Domain 4: Supply Chain / Logistics (SAP, Oracle WMS)

**[SCM #10]**: **Bottleneck Detection**
_Concept_: System automatically analyzes which tasks/persons most often delay the chain. "Marketing photo editing is on average 5 days overdue and blocks 3 follow-up tasks." Shows the most costly bottlenecks in the entire onboarding process.
_Novelty_: Shifts from "who is late" to "what costs us the most delay."

**[SCM #11]**: **Estimated Completion Date**
_Concept_: Based on the task chain and historical lead times, the system calculates an estimated onboarding completion date per starter. "Based on current progress, Kevin's onboarding is expected to be complete on May 15."
_Novelty_: Predictive instead of reactive — HR can intervene proactively.

**[SCM #12]**: **Critical Path Highlighting**
_Concept_: System calculates the critical path — the longest chain of dependent tasks. Those tasks are marked red: if delays occur there, the entire onboarding shifts. Other tasks have "slack."
_Novelty_: Project management thinking applied to HR processes.

### Domain 5: Healthcare Onboarding

**[HC #13]**: **Pre-boarding Readiness Gate**
_Concept_: A starter can only "actively start" when all mandatory pre-boarding tasks are green. Visual checklist: contract ✅, badge photo ✅, IT accounts ✅, safety training ❌. System signals "not ready" until everything is green.
_Novelty_: Combination of chain tasks + readiness check — prevents someone from starting without everything arranged.

**[HC #14]**: **Compliance Deadline Tracker**
_Concept_: Certain tasks have a hard deadline linked to an external obligation (e.g., "badge must be ready 3 days before start date"). System counts down and automatically escalates as deadline approaches.
_Novelty_: Distinction between "nice to have on time" and "must have by deadline."

### Domain 6: Gaming / Gamification

**[GAME #15]**: **Onboarding Achievements**
_Concept_: Small visual rewards when milestones are reached: "All IT tasks complete! 🏆", "Fastest onboarding this month! ⚡". Visible to HR admin and optionally to the starter themselves.
_Novelty_: Makes onboarding less about "checking boxes" and more about a shared success story.

**[GAME #16]**: **Team Leaderboard**
_Concept_: Entities or departments see how they score on onboarding speed, task completion rate, and material coverage. Friendly competition motivates.
_Novelty_: Social proof + competition drives better processes without management pressure.

## Phase 5: QES Alternatives Exploration (2026-04-10)

### Context

SES (Simple Electronic Signature) is implemented and working. QES (Qualified Electronic Signature) is needed as a higher-trust signing option. Itsme was the initial target but may not be practical due to onboarding complexity.

### QES Provider Landscape

| ID | Provider | Type | Cost | Complexity | Notes |
|----|----------|------|------|------------|-------|
| **QES-1** | Itsme (CSC API) | Direct integration | B2B contract | Medium | 6.4M users, strongest brand recognition |
| **QES-2** | myID.be | Belgian QTSP | €980 setup + €0.50/sig | Low-Medium | Transparent pricing, free test env, JSON API |
| **QES-3** | Docusign + Itsme | Platform | ~€25-45/mo + per sig | Low | Docusign manages compliance |
| **QES-4** | SigningHub | EU remote signing | Enterprise pricing | Medium | EAL4+ certified, white-label |
| **QES-5** | Connective | Belgian enterprise | On request | Medium | M365 integration, Power Automate |
| **QES-6** | Belgian eID | Card reader | €15/reader hardware | High | Free per sig, strongest legal basis, poor UX |

### Recommendation

**myID.be** identified as most pragmatic alternative: Belgian, transparent pricing, low barrier to test. A **dual-provider strategy** (myID.be primary + Itsme optional) provides both accessibility and brand recognition.

### Status

Session paused — user wants to explore other ideas first. QES implementation deferred for later evaluation.
